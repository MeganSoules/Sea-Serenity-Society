Marvelous Microbiomes

May 26
Written By Sea Serenity Society

What Is A Microbiome?

Microbiome: The most important aspect to the health of any living organism. This word refers to the collection of microorganisms residing in a specific environment.

The Human Gut Microbiome

The gut microbiome, among other human microbiomes, has essential functions in digestion, nutrient absorption, and immune system function. They assist in the digestion of intricate carbohydrates, production of necessary vitamins, and defense against harmful pathogens. “Evidenced by several studies is the influence of environmental microbes upon the human microbiome and ultimately human health. As the living environment dramatically affects the microbiota, a closer living with nature would facilitate the diversification and balance of microbiota inside the body. A multi-disciplinary understanding, joint effort, and thought system can be the possible solution to obtain optimum health for humans, animals, and the environment.” (Panthee et al., 2022).

Marine Microbiomes & Human Health

Marine ecosystems rely on complex communities of microorganisms in ocean microbiomes for their vital functions. The ocean microbiome has the potential to greatly benefit human health. Nevertheless, disturbances in marine microbial communities, caused by pollution or climate change, can result in imbalances that could bring dangerous pathogens to humans, either through direct water exposure or indirectly through food consumption. A 2022 study discovered that neurotoxins present in marine wildlife can have negative effects on environmental and human health. “... BTX exposure has been interrelated with pathways of carbohydrate and energy metabolism, as well as consequent oxidative stress caused by the toxin. These observed metabolic alterations have been integrated into a model consistent with well-characterized neurotoxicity as well as with pathways that may provide aspects including respiratory and pulmonary effects and associated cellular energetics” (Guillotin & Delcourt, 2022).

Benefits of Ocean Microbiomes

Ocean microbiomes have a considerable influence on human health through diverse mechanisms. These tiny creatures in the ocean provide new substances with medical use, resulting in the creation of fresh antibiotics, antivirals, and anti-cancer drugs. Furthermore, the well-being of ocean microbiomes plays a crucial role in the overall health of marine ecosystems, which are essential sources of food for humans, specifically fish and other seafood, a main protein source for millions of people globally. Moreover, comprehending ocean microbiomes improves our understanding of worldwide biogeochemical cycles and environmental changes, helping anticipate and prevent health risks linked to climate change. The health of human populations is closely connected to the well-being of ocean microbiomes, indicating an intricate relationship between the two.
