Red Tide: How It Affects You

May 27
Written By Sea Serenity Society

Red Tide algae is an issue that not only poses a threat to our coastal wildlife, but to us as well. Red Tide refers to the blooming of Karenia Brevis algae which can mostly be found in the waters of the Gulf of Mexico. In small blooms, K. Brevis algae poses little to no threat. However, in large masses, the spores of this algae can lead to high health risks and even death for not only wildlife but humans as well. A study found in the journal of “Harmful Algae” covers the risks and recorded dangers K. Brevis blooms pose.

Poisoning

Red Tide in a mass quantity can release toxins that infect shellfish and a variety of other fish species. Humans who eat these infected fish can experience infection from toxins related to paralytic or neurotic shellfish poisoning. This can lead to victims contracting gastrointestinal Infections to neurological impairment.

Respiratory Issues

Red Tide blooms are also capable of releasing microscopic spores that can travel by coastal wind. Inhalation of these spores has been noted to cause severe coughing, wheezing, irritation of the throat, as well as difficulty breathing. These spores are exceptionally dangerous to anyone with pre existing respiratory illnesses such as asthma. Prolonged exposure to Red Tide spores have led to reports of permanent lung damage and for those with pre existing issues, death.

Physical Irritation

Exposure to Red Tide algae, direct or airborne, have led to victims experiencing physical pain and discomfort. Reports of eye irritation, inflammation of the nasal cavity, extreme rash and a painful burning sensation are results of exposure. Anyone swimming in Infected water or standing near the water line are likely to be heavily affected and are urged to seek immediate medical attention. Over exposure to airborne spores without proper treatment could lead to prolonged health risk such as partial blindness or lung damage.

Beyond Physical

Red Tide not only affects wildlife and human health, but also the economic health of it's location. Red Tide is in a Sense, a disease, therefore it must be treated with the utmost caution. Infected waters can lead to beach closers for prolonged periods of time as well as entire towns shutting down due to air pollution. Tourism is greatly affected by this and certain places that rely on that seasonal income begin to suffer. Most notably in places that see increased population growth during the summer months. Red Tide has led to severe drops in revenue greatly damaging or even killing off entire businesses.

How Can You Help?

Recognizing Red Tide is the first step in helping to treat it, thanks to a number of active organizations we can keep our coastline healthy and safe for years to come. Below are some sources for more information on Red Tide and how you can help.
